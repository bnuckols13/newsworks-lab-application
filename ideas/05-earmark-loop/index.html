The pitch

The hook

$6,980,756 landed in seven Allegheny County projects on March 28, 2025, under the state Redevelopment Assistance Capital Program. RACP awards are line-items, written by a legislator into the state capital budget before the Office of the Budget ever scores them. Two public records describe that world, the award spreadsheets and the Schedule A campaign-finance filings, and nobody has joined them.

The story

The reporting builds one merged dataset, western-PA RACP awards against contributions to the legislators who sponsored them, and tests for a single ordering: money in, then the earmark. Where a grantee's principals gave to a sponsoring legislator before that legislator's budget carried their line-item, the pattern is the story. I want to name the loop and document it to the dollar and the date, and I do not want to allege an exchange or a motive, because the records show sequence and sum, not intent.

Why now

The capital-budget cycle is live and RACP-transparency bills are moving in Harrisburg. The awards get covered once, as a legislator's press release. The donation-then-earmark join is absent from all of it.

Why you

I built this method already, on the PrimeCare-to-Chester donor loop: match the money in against the money out, publish the table, hold the quid pro quo open. Every source ships archived and SHA-256 hashed, so a reader re-verifies the match table with no login and no trust in me.

The reporting plan

The merge is a build the reporting generates, not a finding I hold. One hard problem is honest up front: the release announces the package, and announcement is not sponsorship, so attributing an earmark to its actual budget sponsor requires a Right-to-Know request to the Office of the Budget for some line-items. Every named legislator and grantee gets right of reply before publication.

Logistics

One legislator and one package per bite, with the cross-district match table as the enterprise feature the bites accrue toward. The first bite is a single scoped-and-verified pair, reply solicited.

The close

The award list is public. The donor list is public. The state has never been asked to explain what happens when you read them in order.

The discipline

This is the hottest of the five, so it carries the strictest tier discipline: name the loop, never the deal. The records show the money in, the money out, and the ordering; the quid pro quo stays an open, documented question. No intent verbs, only loop, ordering, sequence, sum. The matched pair is a build, never a completed match asserted here, and any adverse pair triggers the 72-hour right-of-reply hold before it appears anywhere.
