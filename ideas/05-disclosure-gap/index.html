Story idea 05 · conflict of interest

The Disclosure Gap

Every local official in Pennsylvania files one sworn page each May: a Statement of Financial Interests, listing the businesses and income that could pull against the public duty. The law writes the questions and refiles them every year across 2,500-plus municipalities. Almost no one reads them against how the same official then votes.

Confirmed the sworn-form law, the state aid, the audit findings   Build whether a given official's form discloses the interest, and the abstention record

The build so far

One sworn one-page form, filed locally under the Public Official and Employee Ethics Act (65 Pa.C.S. §§ 1101–1113), turns into a repeatable test in any of the county's 130 municipalities. Every target is a candidate, not a finding: the structure is public, but whether the official disclosed the interest and recused is what pulling the form, the check register, and the minutes settles.

The pitch

The hook

In Pitcairn, the borough council president also serves as chief of the volunteer fire organization his council funds, and the state aid that flows to that organization is allocated through a borough whose recent Auditor General review recorded four compliance findings under his presidency. Every one of those facts is public. The one document that would show whether the arrangement was disclosed and recused, his Statement of Financial Interests, has not yet been read against his votes.

The story

One sworn one-page form, the Statement of Financial Interests every Pennsylvania local official files each May, tested against the public money the same official then votes on. The claim is not that any official is corrupt. The claim is measurable against a statutory form: where an official's business or income is paid by the body he sits on, did the form disclose it, and did the member abstain? The finding is the dataset, official by official, of disclosed-and-recused against undisclosed-or-unrecused, built one municipality at a time.

Why now

The form refiles every May 1, so the beat renews itself annually with no news peg required. Federal and state money is moving through small municipal and authority budgets that carry little scrutiny, which is exactly where a part-time official's outside interest and a public appropriation can meet.

Why you

I built this method already and scouted all three tiers of Allegheny County against it. Every form, register, and set of minutes ships archived and SHA-256 hashed, so a reader re-verifies each case with no login and no trust in me. The method is the product: it points at any of Pennsylvania's 2,500-plus municipalities.

The reporting plan

Scouted: the candidate structures across the county, the aid flows, the audit findings. The build, and the actual work: pull each official's SFI, match it to the appropriation votes and the abstention record, and tier every case. A self-proving case, the form omits an interest the register documents, can publish; a corrupt-benefit read stays a documented question with full right of reply.

Logistics

A story a week, one municipality at a time, with the county disclosure matrix as the enterprise spine the bites accrue toward. The reporting leaves the building for borough meetings and the officials themselves. The archive hands off, so a partner newsroom can take a county and run the same form-against-votes test.

The close

The Ethics Act already requires the form, already asks the questions, and already refiles them every May. What it does not do is read the answer against the vote. That reading is a story a week, sitting in a filing cabinet in every borough building in the county.

The discipline

Every target is a candidate until the form is read; none is a finding. The story tests officials against a sworn statutory form, and it names an official only where the public record supports the test and after right of reply. A self-proving omission can run; a read that the disclosed interest produced a corrupt benefit stays a documented question. No official is called corrupt on the strength of a structure alone.
