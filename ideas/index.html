Five story ideas · western Pennsylvania

Five ideas, one engine

Each is a reusable public-records production line pointed at the region: a bond filing, a payroll journal, a lien dataset, a capital-budget line-item, a campaign-finance database. Every source archived and hashed so a reader re-verifies it with no network and no keys.

01 · Flagship · captured revenue math

Can the airport pay for its terminal?

A debt-service-coverage stress test on $1.7 billion in bonds against four revenue pillars that are each, for different reasons, less certain than the debt they secure: gaming set-asides, Marcellus wells past peak, expiring federal money, and passenger growth that has not arrived.

How these were built

The method, and the honesty of it

Each idea was scored on how publishable and how under-reported it is, then written to full editor-pitch grade. Each ships its confirmed public starter data; anything the reporting would generate is marked a build, never asserted as a finding.

Two tiers, kept visible

Every fact on these pages is one of two kinds, and the page says which. Confirmed means a public record that exists right now, archived and citable. Build means the dataset the reporting would generate: a cross-reference, a match, a scored table that does not exist until the requests go out. No page asserts a finding it has not produced, names a victim it has not verified, or alleges an exchange the records do not show. Institutions are framed as institutions and owed right of reply; where a quid pro quo is the question, it stays a documented question.
