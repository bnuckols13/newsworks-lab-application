Five story ideas · western Pennsylvania

Five story ideas

Different beats, one habit: start from a public record, build the dataset the story needs, and publish the sources so a reader can check the work. Two could move in the first week; all five are western Pennsylvania.

01 · Flagship · captured revenue math

Can the airport pay for its terminal?

A debt-service-coverage stress test on $1.7 billion in bonds against four revenue pillars that are each, for different reasons, less certain than the debt they secure: gaming set-asides, Marcellus wells past peak, expiring federal money, and passenger growth that has not arrived.

02 · Follow the money

The Alignment Map

City Council, County Council, the mayor, the county executive: what they vote and fund, set against what donors and registered lobbyists want, set against what residents say they want. The gaps among the three are the story.

Open the build

03 · Hidden harm to bodies

Hours Per Resident

CMS payroll-verified staffing joined to Care Compare harm outcomes for every Allegheny nursing home, tested for a case-mix-adjusted gradient. Correlation, not cause, and named homes get right of reply.

Open the build

04 · Automated policing

The Automated Suspect

Local, state, and federal law enforcement across western Pennsylvania are buying face recognition, license-plate readers, and real-time crime feeds. The story maps which agency runs which tool, and where an automated output moves a case with no human checking it.

Open the build

05 · Conflict of interest

The Disclosure Gap

Every Pennsylvania local official files one sworn Statement of Financial Interests each May. Read against the public money the same official then votes on, the form becomes a story a week across the county's 130 municipalities.

How these were built

How I picked them, and how honest they are

I checked each one for how publishable it is and how little anyone else has done it, then wrote it up the way I'd pitch an editor. Each carries the public records it would start from, and it says which facts are confirmed and which the reporting still has to produce.

Two tiers, kept visible

Every fact on these pages is one of two kinds, and the page says which. Confirmed means a public record that exists right now, archived and citable. Build means the dataset the reporting would generate: a cross-reference, a match, a scored table that does not exist until the requests go out. No page asserts a finding it has not produced, names a victim it has not verified, or alleges an exchange the records do not show. Institutions are framed as institutions and owed right of reply; where a quid pro quo is the question, it stays a documented question.
