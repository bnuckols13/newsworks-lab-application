The application, on one page

A cover letter, a portfolio, three reproducible work samples, and five western-Pennsylvania story ideas.

Every sample and every idea runs on one engine: a reusable public-records production line that turns a sworn form, a bond filing, a fee schedule, a payroll journal, or a capital-budget line-item into a repeatable series, with each source archived and SHA-256 hashed so a reader re-verifies it with no network and no keys. That is open-source journalism in the sense the lab's mission names.

Five story ideas

Each idea ships the engine and its confirmed public starter data. These are prospective pitches: the builds show the method and the records, and frame anything the reporting would generate as a build, never a finding.

Flagship · debt vs. revenue

Can the airport pay for its terminal?

A stress test on $1.7B in bonds against four shaky revenue pillars: gaming set-asides, Marcellus wells past peak, expiring federal money, and passenger growth that has not arrived.
