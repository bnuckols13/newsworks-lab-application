Cover letter Enterprise Journalist · Newsworks Lab Brian Nuckols

Hi Andrew,

Thank you for opening this role. What you're building at Newsworks Lab, open-source, data-driven, and focused on source-driven, community-centered reporting, is inspiring! I'd love to help build it as your next enterprise reporter.

In the rest of this letter, I'd like to connect some of my previous reporting to those values, and show what that approach could produce for the lab.

Let me start with a recent piece on Pittsburgh Water that appeared at Public Source. I was covering a board meeting in late 2025 and I was struck by how much was moving with no discussion. The board made decisions worth millions with no roll call and no comment. I found myself curious about the substance behind the various votes, but the process moved so fast that I barely had time to write things down.

That experience led to a multi-pronged investigation, including a coded database of two years of meeting transcripts representing just under a billion dollars in spending. My reporting found that over that period there were exactly zero "no" votes: 223 of 228 resolutions passed unanimously, many without any discussion or public record.

Then I started calling sources: residents who pay the bills, officials inside the utility, elected officials, and subject-matter experts who could verify the technical side of board governance. I confirmed that Pittsburgh Water was improperly using executive sessions to deliberate off the record, and numerous board members told me the practice needs to stop. At the very next meeting the board asked 40 questions, roughly seven times its two-year average, and issued a stop-work order.

I wanted to share that reporting because I think it's a microcosm of how I work. I like to connect open-source public records, data journalism, and in-depth sourced reporting into a complete story that surfaces the issues that matter to a community. I've run that same engine twice more this year in western PA: on the city's inclusionary zoning debate and on the new $1.75 billion airport terminal. My full portfolio is here.

To better explain my method, I'd like to point to a few of my heroes, the reporters who show me both what I do and what I want to do better: ProPublica, the ICIJ, and recent investigative Pulitzers like Eric Eyre's opioid-shipment reporting and the Wall Street Journal's work on officials trading the stocks they regulate. Reading them, I noticed a pattern of at least four things they all do, which I now use as a checklist for my own work: build a dataset that doesn't exist yet instead of relaying a document someone handed me; go after an institution that failed a duty, not just one bad actor; hold the wrong up against a specific rule, so it's documented and not just my opinion; and point to a real result at the end.

I've attached five story ideas I built with that checklist, all of them western PA. I'd rather walk you through them in person, but each one comes ready the way I'd want to work at the lab: a short read on who's already covered the angle and who hasn't, the first people I'd call, the documents and numbers that would have to survive my fact-checking, the person at the center of it, the result it could force, and why now. One asks whether the county's airport authority can actually service the $1.7 billion in bonds behind its new terminal, stress-testing four revenue streams that are each, for different reasons, less certain than the debt they secure. Another maps what the region's elected bodies fund against what their donors and registered lobbyists want and what residents say they want, so the gaps among the three become the story.

What pulled me toward this job in particular is the collaboration and the room to try things, move fast, and learn from what doesn't work. I report from documents and data, and I'm also good at explaining complicated things to people who aren't paid to care about them, in writing and on camera. That's the reporter I'd be for Newsworks Lab, and I'd be glad to hand the whole verifiable package to a partner newsroom whenever it helps the region.

Thanks for building this, and for reading. I'd love to talk!

Brian Nuckols
